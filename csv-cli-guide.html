2. Explore your data

Let's say you have a CSV file called employees.csv with columns: name, age, salary, department.

First, take a quick look at the table in a readable format:

4. Filter rows by column value

Need to find all Engineering employees? csv-filter makes it simple:

$ evtool csv-filter employees.csv --column department --value Engineering
name,age,salary,department
Alice,30,75000,Engineering
Bob,25,68000,Engineering
Eve,32,78000,Engineering

Want the opposite — everyone except Engineering?

$ evtool csv-filter employees.csv --column department --value Engineering --invert
name,age,salary,department
Carol,35,82000,Sales
Dave,28,72000,Sales
Frank,45,95000,Management
Grace,29,71000,Sales

Filters output clean CSV that you can pipe directly into the next tool.

5. Sort your records

Sort by any column, numeric or text, ascending or descending:

$ evtool csv-sort employees.csv --column salary --reverse --numeric
name,age,salary,department
Frank,45,95000,Management
Carol,35,82000,Sales
Eve,32,78000,Engineering
Alice,30,75000,Engineering
Dave,28,72000,Sales
Grace,29,71000,Sales
Bob,25,68000,Engineering

Combine with csv-head to show the top N by a metric:

$ evtool csv-sort employees.csv --column salary -r -n | evtool csv-head -n 3
name,age,salary,department
Frank,45,95000,Management
Carol,35,82000,Sales
Eve,32,78000,Engineering

8. Merge multiple CSV files

When you have related data across files, csv-merge joins them on a common key — like SQL JOIN for the terminal:

$ evtool csv-merge employees.csv departments.csv --on department
(Inner join on the "department" column — only matching rows)

Supports inner, left, and outer joins. Works like a relational database join, but with zero setup.

9. Compare CSV files

Need to find what changed between two versions of a CSV? diff-csv shows you:

$ evtool diff-csv employees-v1.csv employees-v2.csv
Shows added, removed, and changed rows — colorized output included.

10. Real workflow: put it all together

Here's a complete real-world workflow. Let's say you need to answer: "What's the average salary of Engineering employees, and how does it compare to Sales?"

# Step 1: Filter Engineering employees
$ evtool csv-filter employees.csv --column department --value Engineering > eng.csv

# Step 2: Analyze their salaries
$ evtool csv-stats eng.csv
Shows mean salary: 73,666.67 for Engineering

# Step 3: Same for Sales
$ evtool csv-filter employees.csv --column department --value Sales | evtool csv-stats
Shows mean salary: 73,333.33 for Sales

# Step 4: Visualize the department headcount comparison
$ evtool chart-cli pie 3 3 1
Shows: Engineering 42.9%, Sales 42.9%, Management 14.3%

# Step 5: Save the filtered data as JSON for further processing
$ evtool csv2json eng.csv > eng.json

All of this — filtering, analyzing, visualizing, converting — without opening Excel, without writing a Python script, without installing pandas.
